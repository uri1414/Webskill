Why AirScout

One snapshot. Every pixel relative.

We are the only aerial imagery company that captures your entire field in a single shot. That's the foundation of a prescription you can trust. Everyone else flies a grid and stitches the tiles together, which distorts the very differences you're trying to manage.

AirScout · Single capture

The whole field, in one frame

Because every pixel is exposed in the same instant, values stay true relative to each other across the entire field. That relationship is what makes a good zone.

Others · Grid and stitch

Tiles flown minutes apart

Grid patterns capture sections under changing light and temperature, then stitch them. Seams and drift creep in, making the imagery unsuitable for real prescriptions.

Built for prescriptions

Our technology

Two patented ways to read a living field

AirScout is the proactive crop-management tool. It's easy to learn, practical to use, and works seamlessly with the precision equipment you already run.

AirScout Thermal Imagery

Sensitive to 3/100° C
  • Shows where plants run a "stress fever" from any factor, biotic or abiotic.
  • Reveals stress far sooner than reflective technologies, and reads bare-soil conditions.
  • Detects where to intervene weeks ahead of serious crop damage, not days.
  • Creates precision treatment files that drive your VRT equipment directly.
Thermal · plant stress fever

AirScout ADVI Imagery

Advanced vegetation index
  • Advances standard NDVI imaging to the next level of detail.
  • A wider range of color for higher-resolution, more legible field maps.
  • Reads relative nutrition needs across the field by foliage reflectance.
  • The Prescription Tool puts application decisions in your hands, or your agronomist's.
ADVI · relative plant nutrition

Our story

Born in a Piper Cub. Refined over thirty years.

AirScout started in the '90s when our founder, Brian, needed accurate zones to make his own precision equipment work with GPS. What you see today is the culmination of decades of learning that began with a couple of farm kids taking pictures out of an airplane window. We're still farmers, and we're still learning.
